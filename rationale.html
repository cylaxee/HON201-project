Rationale

Questions:

How are the diets of students at PSU affected by their busy schedules?
Do students who cook for themselves consider themselves to have a healthier diet than students who do not cook for themselves?

Claim:

Students who have a lighter schedule and cook for themselves will have a healthier diet than students with busier schedules and do not cook for themselves.

Hypothesis:

Students who cook for themselves and have a lighter schedule will probably have a healthier diet because they have a greater opportunity to attend to their health than the students with busier schedules and do not cook for themselves.

If the topic of my hypothesis is confirmed, it would suggest that being less busy and cooking for oneself allows one to have a relatively “healthier lifestyle.” This suggested relationship may encourage students to attempt to allot more time to attending to their dietary needs.

If the topic of my hypothesis is disconfirmed, it would suggest that there is not a relationship between the busyness of a student’s schedule and the health of the individual based on their diet.
